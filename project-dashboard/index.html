Abstract
Neuromorphic biomemristor
Overview
Memristive synapse for neuromorphic computing
Neuromorphic computing models brain-like processing by combining memory and computation in one element. A memristor adjusts resistance based on past input, enabling synaptic plasticity. Gelatin and honey act as ionic, low-power, eco-friendly materials for simple neuromorphic devices. AI hardware consumes high energy, creating scalability limits for future systems. Global e-waste exceeds 50 million tonnes per year, demanding sustainable alternatives. Bio-memristors offer low-cost, green hardware synapses for efficient neuromorphic computing.
Switching Performance
Key resistive switching metrics
Vset
SET threshold
Switching to low-resistance state
0.21 – 3.0 V
Typical SET voltages extracted from I–V hysteresis loops of organic and honey-based memristors span from about 0.21 V and 0.23 V up to 0.6 V, 1.0 V, 2.0 V and 3.0 V, depending on the device stack and active layer thickness.
Vreset
RESET threshold
Switching to high-resistance state
−0.04 – −3.0 V
RESET operation drives the device back to the high-resistance state with typical voltages between −0.04 V and −3.0 V, again identified from the bipolar I–V switching curves of gelatin CNT, honey and carbon-dot based devices.
ON/OFF ratio
ION / IOFF
Dynamic resistance window
10² – 10⁶
Across reference devices, ON/OFF current ratios span from 10²–10⁴ for gelatin-CNT and honey films up to 10⁶ for carbon-dot and honey-CNT structures, providing a large conductance window for multi-level synaptic weights.
Endurance
Switching cycles
Cycling stability of organic memristors
35 – 10⁶ cycles
Reported endurance varies with material system: gelatin-CNT devices are cycled for about 35 switching events, honey-CNT devices sustain more than 10³ cycles, and carbon dot based memories can reach up to 10⁶ cycles in the best cases.
Retention
State stability
Non-volatile conductance retention
10⁴ – 10⁶ s
Typical retention measurements show stable conductance for around 10⁴ s, while carbon-dot devices reach 10⁵–10⁶ s and some honey-based synapses extrapolate to multi-year stability under read voltages of about 0.1–0.2 V.
Device Physics
I–V analysis & mechanisms
Log(I) – Log(V)
Slope analysis
Extracted slopes from I–V characteristics
1.17 & 3.21
In log–log I–V plots, low-voltage conduction shows a slope of about 1.17, close to ohmic behaviour, while the high-voltage regime exhibits a slope around 3.21, associated with trap-filled space-charge-limited conduction, consistent with the classical J ∝ V²/L³ framework.
Conduction mechanism
Identified mode
Ohmic to trap-filled SCLC transition
Ohmic → TF-SCLC
The combined slope analysis indicates a transition from an ohmic region at low bias (slope ≈ 1) through space-charge-limited conduction (slope ≈ 2) into a trap-filled limit with slopes greater than 3, matching the behaviour measured in honey and gelatin CNT membranes.
Neuromorphic Metrics
Synaptic performance
PPF index
Short-term plasticity
Paired-pulse facilitation behaviour
≈ 370 %
In paired-pulse experiments, the second excitatory post-synaptic current can reach about 370 % of the first for an inter-pulse interval Δt ≈ 20 ms, with fitted double-exponential decay constants in the millisecond and tens-of-milliseconds ranges.
Conductance states
Analog synapse
Multi-level honey memristor
256 levels
The honey-based synaptic device supports 256 distinct conductance levels, enabling fine-grained weight tuning in the neuromorphic system and allowing accurate mapping of analog weight updates during learning.
NL LTP
Potentiation non-linearity
Long-term potentiation curve fit
+0.01
Fitting the conductance-versus-pulse-number curve for long-term potentiation gives a non-linearity parameter NL-LTP ≈ +0.01, indicating nearly linear incremental increases in synaptic weight with repeated programming pulses.
NL LTD
Depression non-linearity
Long-term depression curve fit
−1.93
The corresponding long-term depression branch yields NL-LTD ≈ −1.93, reflecting a strongly non-linear decrease in conductance during weight erasure, consistent with the 256-state honey memristor synapse used in the neuromorphic system.
